Getting a Partner for Data Services

by Admin | Category big data | Sept 16th, 2021

1 Comments

When it comes to use of data to spur organizational growth, everyone needs a cost effective solution offered by a partner that can deliver quality results with agility. This is very important because the rules of business keep changing, customer needs keep evolving, competition keeps increasing and business environment gets complex each day. Many firms may want to process one-time data which is a good idea, but business is an on-going process which requires expediency, scalability and efficiency as key factors for growth. These factors can easily be achieved by firms with reliable partners to handle data.

Scalability which is basically the capability to leverage both cutting-edge technology and provide the manpower needed to help you grow is essential but is often a challenge for most organizations. This is true because building a workforce internally or externally is difficult and costly. Infact, scaling that workforce simply becomes impossible which means, get data work to be done internally is a fete that can rarely be achieved by many firms.

The Changing Face of Unstructured Data

We are living in a world where the amount of data created rises by 100% every 18 months and many organizations rarely make meaning of these data. This means that vast majority of organizations store almost 80% of unstructured data with the potential to help them transform; but since it is not unstructured, it remains useless. The rise of this unstructured data is mostly driven by relatively new technological forces known as SMAC (social, mobile, analytics and cloud). Unstructured data presents both challenges and new business opportunities for many organizations.

Challenges of Unstructured Data

Many organizations may not realize this, but unstructured data presents huge additional costs for running businesses. These costs are hidden in time wasted when looking for information, wasted storage capacity (cloud system, internal computers, and servers). It also presents potential legal exposures if data cannot be located; this is the case for many government agencies who lack professional partners that can help them manage data digitally.

The information contained in unstructured data is not easy to locate, infact, employees in data-rich companies spend well between 30 – 35% of their time and knowledge looking for information from unstructured data. Surprisingly, 50% of the time, they never get this information. This implies that they either have to recreate it (often impossible) or just deal with the reality of missing critical information.

This poses serious risks especially in departments where there are regulatory requirements that mandate record keeping. Some of the departments that bear greatest risk of potential litigation because of unstructured data such as Human Resources, Finance and Production must have their data readily structured for consumption.

Opportunities in Unstructured Data

Key areas of knowledge requisite for any organization are hidden in unstructured data. Because building an internal workforce to take care of data is expensive (money, scalability and time) as well as difficult (it’s not easy to bring together individuals with required knowledge and skills), many firms are not able to extract this knowledge and make it actionable. However, with the right partner to manage data, organizations can be able to apply this knowledge to:

  • Improve management
  • Establish key information about their customers
  • Establish vital insights about employee potential
  • Identify new markets and increase sales
  • Adopt new marketing strategies
  • Establish their market share and improve general organizational growth
    • The benefits presented by such knowledge outweighs the cost of managing this data if the organization finds a right partner with the tools to fast and effectively structure data and deliver this information. But is important for organizations first know what information they want to extract from the unstructured data and how they will use it. A survey conducted by Research Solutions Network on various multinational firms in Kenya revealed that 95% of them have made deliberate choice to outsource the challenging data management work to relevant data firms. This has made them successful in structuring all their data in real time and using it in making essential business decisions.

    It has also enabled them to focus their attention on their core competencies. This is important because no company wants to do what was not meant for them since it leads to increased business operation costs and diverts focus from innovation and improving customer experience. Indeed, the difference between successful business and one that is not is how well it focuses on its core competency. This underscores the significance of identifying relevant partners with the right tools to take care of your other business needs to spur growth.
